Drake Maye: Early Life

Drake Maye

Family Life

Drake Maye was born on August 30, 2002 in Huntersville, North Carolina. Drake was born into the very athletic Maye family. His father Mark Maye also played quarterback at the Univeristy of Norht Carolina in the 1980's. Mark then went on to play for the Tampa Bay Buccanerrs in the NFL. Mark's time in the NFL was brief however, as he quickly moved on to play for the Raleigh-Durham Seahawks in the World League of American Football (WLAF). Drake is the youngest of 4 brothers who also had distinguished college careers. His brothers Luke and Beau played basketball at UNC, where they won the 2017 National Championship with Luke hitting a buzzer beater in the Elite 8. His other brother Cole played college baseball at the Univeristy of Florida and went on to win the 2017 College World Series. Drake grew up with fellow NFL quarterback Mason Rudolph, as their fathers played together at UNC. Maye's family pedigree is second to none.

High School

Maye started his high school career at William A. Hough High School in Cornelius, North Carolina. He played there for one season adn then transfered for his sophomore year to Myers Park High School in Charlotte, North Carolina. Drake played football and basketball in High School abnd excelled at both sports. As a Junior in High School he received All-Conference and All-District honors in Basketball and was named the Charlotte observers 2019 male athlete of the year in football. Maye threw for 3.512 yards and 50 touchdowns leading his team to a conference championship appearance.

Maye was named an Under Armour All-American in 2020 despite not being able to play his senior season due to the Covid-19 pandemic that shook the United States. May earned a 4 star rating as a colege football prospect and was attracting interest from some of the highest quality college football programs in the Nation. Maye originally commiteed to play college football at the University of Alabama under legendary head coach Nick Saban. However, Maye decided not to attend Alabama and play football for the Crimson Tide and instead flipped his commitment to the Univeristy of North Carolina to play football in his home state. Maye made this decision to become a Tar Heel in March of 2020 and quickly found himself on the Chapel Hill campus pursuing his football dreams.
